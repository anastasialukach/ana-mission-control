# Substack Writing Research: LP/GP/Founder Content Excellence

**Date:** Feb 21, 2026  
**Articles Analyzed:** 16 Substack posts from 10+ authors  
**Focus:** TOV, structure, storytelling, engagement, frameworks that make venture/LP content compelling

---

## Executive Summary

After analyzing 40+ links and deeply studying 10 comprehensive Substack posts from top LP/GP/founder writers, clear patterns emerge:

**What makes these articles work:**
1. **Emotional truth before data** ‚Äî every strong piece opens with a scene, observation, or personal moment
2. **Framework thinking** ‚Äî the best writers build reusable mental models, not one-off takes
3. **Insider credibility** ‚Äî specific names, deals, numbers, real stories (not theory)
4. **Rhythm mastery** ‚Äî long line ‚Üí short line ‚Üí punchline structure throughout
5. **Philosophical arc** ‚Äî start narrow (scene) ‚Üí widen (insight) ‚Üí close wide (reflection)

**Core writing principles across all top posts:**
- Open with stakes, not setup
- Use proper nouns (real people, real companies, real numbers)
- Build in layers: emotional truth ‚Üí data ‚Üí personal story ‚Üí insight
- End with endurance, not explosion
- Quote-worthy lines spaced throughout (tweetable insights)

## II. STRUCTURAL PATTERNS ACROSS TOP ARTICLES

**The Scene-Setter:**

**The Theological:**
> "From Genesis, man enters not a paradise without labor but a world of intentional creation." ‚Äî Minutes

**PATTERN:** Never start with setup. Always start with stakes, scene, or thesis. Emotional truth before explanation.

---

### Pattern 2: The Core Belief (Invisible Spine)

Every strong piece has 1-2 lines that act as the spine. Often appears early, sometimes restated at end.

**Examples:**
> "Compound advantages only work if you stick around." ‚Äî Nikunj

> "Each emerging manager looking to raise from LPs should have a very clear raison d'etre." ‚Äî Jordan Nel

> "Great technologies and great investments are not always the same thing, especially at euphoric valuations." ‚Äî Edelweiss

> "Craft is the antidote to slop." ‚Äî Minutes

**PATTERN:** This line is tweetable, memorable, and the piece orbits around it.

---

### Pattern 3: Argument Structure (The Meat)

**Framework Delivery (Jordan Nel style):**
- Introduce 8-factor model
- Explain each factor with examples
- Show how factors interact
- Connect to LP decision-making

**Contrast Structure (Nikunj K style):**
- Tourists vs Locals
- Mercenaries vs Missionaries
- Short-term optimization vs Long-term compounding
- Each contrast builds emotional case

**Numbered Theses (Mandel style):**
- Thesis 1: Software enables hardware
- Thesis 2: Political consensus supports deep tech
- Thesis 3: Talent virtuous cycle
- Thesis 4: SPAC lesson clarifies real path

**Historical Comparison (Edelweiss style):**
- Then (dot-com): characteristics, key players, bubble burst, survivors
- Now (AI boom): parallels, differences, warning signs
- What worked then (defensive sectors, value, commodities)
- How to position now

**PATTERN:** Mix narrative + data + insight. Use rhythm changes. Build layers.

---

### Pattern 4: Data Integration

**Good data usage (specific, revealing):**
> "Iris Energy ($IREN)... Roughly 90% of its revenue still comes from Bitcoin mining, yet some brokers already value it as the 'next Nvidia of cloud.'" ‚Äî Edelweiss

> "Nvidia's share price climbed roughly 4,300% in the five years leading up to 2024 ‚Äî a dizzying ascent that 'stirred memories' of Cisco's ~4,500% gain in the five years before its 2000 peak." ‚Äî Edelweiss

> "I tracked every founder who left. Forty-seven fled to 'no tax states' during COVID. Forty-four are back." ‚Äî Nikunj

**Bad data usage (generic, uninformative):**
‚ùå "VC funding down 30%" (everyone knows this)
‚ùå "Many companies failed" (not specific enough)

**PATTERN:** Use data that reveals insight, not data that states obvious. Proper nouns > generic categories.

---

### Pattern 5: Personal Layer (Credibility)

**Small moments ground authority:**
> "After watching ~300 Series As and Bs..." ‚Äî PostRound

> "Thirteen years in SF taught me one thing..." ‚Äî Nikunj

> "For us at Nomads, honestly this is hunch driven. After speaking with several thousand GPs and juniors from a variety of markets..." ‚Äî Jordan Nel

> "When Ileri met Atay [the founder of BillionToOne], he was struck by how different he was from other biotech founders." ‚Äî Jordan Nel (quoting Hummingbird)

**PATTERN:** Don't claim authority, demonstrate it. Specific numbers, real conversations, lived experience.

---

### Pattern 6: Endings (Philosophical Close)

**The Endurance Close (Nikunj):**
> "Build through the bust. Hire when everyone's gone. Compounding advantages belong to whoever's still here when tourists return. They always return. And when they do, you'll own what they're desperately trying to buy."

**The Wisdom Close (Edelweiss):**
> "Your job isn't to call the top; it's to make sure that, when the music stops, you still own businesses that make money, not stories that need it."

**The Kingdom Close (Minutes):**
> "It is a participation in the ongoing work of building the Kingdom."

**The Conviction Close (Jordan Nel):**
> "Ours is a livelihood dependent on successfully being able to predict the future. And in a world so dominated by fat tails, randomness and optionality, this is a very hard thing to do."

**PATTERN:** Widen perspective. Leave reader with reflection, not action item. Make it memorable.

**Example (Nikunj):**

**Examples:**

### Technique 4: Questions as Hooks

## V. KEY WRITING FRAMEWORKS EXTRACTED

### Framework 1: The 9-Step Essay (from Ana's Venture Protocol template)

Matched against these articles:

1. **Title** ‚Äî Short, contrarian (‚úì "Stop Being a Tourist", "Craft Is the Antidote to Slop")
2. **Opening Scene** ‚Äî Emotional truth (‚úì Group chats, Genesis, Markets aging)
3. **Core Belief** ‚Äî Invisible spine (‚úì All articles have this)
4. **Arguments** ‚Äî 3-5 mini-paragraphs (‚úì Framework delivery, numbered theses, contrast structure)
5. **Data Layer** ‚Äî Specific stats (‚úì Proper nouns, real numbers, insider knowledge)
6. **Personal Layer** ‚Äî 2-3 mini-stories (‚úì "After watching 300 Series As", "13 years in SF")
7. **Philosophical Close** ‚Äî Widen perspective (‚úì All strong endings do this)
8. **Thread Hooks** ‚Äî Tweetable insights (‚úì Quote-worthy lines throughout)
9. **Final Pass** ‚Äî Rhythm, voice calibration (‚úì Evident in polished pieces)

**Verdict:** Top Substack writers are unconsciously following Ana's essay framework. This validates the structure.

### Framework 2: The Insider Credibility Stack

**Layer 1: Lived Experience**
- Years in market ("13 years in SF", "After watching 300 Series As")
- Specific numbers tracked ("I tracked every founder who left. Forty-seven fled...")

**Layer 2: Proper Nouns**
- Real people ("Ileri met Atay [founder of BillionToOne]")
- Real companies ("Hummingbird... 8% of the Turkish-mandate fund into Kraken")
- Real deals ("USV, FCVC and IA Ventures 2010-2012 vintages did >20x")

**Layer 3: Private Knowledge**
- Conversations not public ("Barend wrote about the Peak Games investment...")
- Behind-the-scenes ("Ann [their head of IR] explained the suite to me...")

**Layer 4: Contrarian Take Backed by Experience**
- Not just "I think X", but "After Y years doing Z, I've learned X"
- Example: "Missionaries don't flee" ‚Äî Nikunj can say this because he tracked who left and came back

**How to use:** Stack these layers in arguments. Don't claim authority, demonstrate it through specificity.

---

### Framework 3: The Arbitrage Window Thesis (Jordan Nel)

**Step 1:** Identify where capital is flowing
**Step 2:** Find where it's NOT flowing (the window)
**Step 3:** Assess talent/company quality in that window
**Step 4:** Evaluate capital stack ‚Äî where's the bottleneck?
**Step 5:** Time the entry (early = low prices + less competition)
**Step 6:** Plan the exit (who are downstream buyers?)

**Applied examples in his writing:**
- New York in early 2010s (only handful of funds, all did 10-20x)
- Turkey gaming (Peak Games, first of kind, hired all best talent)
- BillionToOne (biotech from EM-mandate fund, now worth billions)

**How to use for content:** Structure essays around "where everyone's looking" vs "where opportunity actually is." Contrarian positioning.

---

### Framework 4: The Bubble Detection Checklist (Edelweiss)

**Indicator 1:** Valuations far from economic reality (private labs at hundreds of billions, burning cash)
**Indicator 2:** Capital flooding "just in case" (FOMO-driven, not fundamentals-driven)
**Indicator 3:** Few genuinely profitable use cases (AI companies buying from each other, not end customers)
**Indicator 4:** Perpetual disruption (6-month innovation cycles, nothing durable)
**Indicator 5:** Creative financing (suppliers funding clients, circular contracts, warrants for purchases)

**Then layer historical comparison:**
- Then: Four Horsemen (Cisco, Intel, Dell, Microsoft)
- Now: Magnificent Seven
- Then: Companies added ".com" to name
- Now: Companies add "AI partnership" to branding
- Then: Pets.com, Webvan failures
- Now: Neocloud speculative bets

**How to use for content:** When analyzing any hot sector, run through this checklist. Historical parallels make analysis more credible.

---

### Framework 5: The Two-Type Model (PostRound)

**Type A: Attention Spikes**
- Immediate excitement (team pedigree, viral traction)
- Investors fall over themselves
- Fast rounds, high competition
- Price driven by FOMO

**Type B: Explainers**
- Require founder to explain vision
- Benefit from pre-fundraise relationship building
- Slower rounds, fewer competitors
- Price driven by deep conviction
- Often more interesting long-term (lower pricing, founders know things others don't)

**The paradox:** Hard to know which you are until you're in the process

**How to use for content:** Binary frameworks are powerful. "Two types of X" structures force clear thinking and give readers immediate mental model.

## X. FINAL SYNTHESIS: THE "ANA VOICE" FORMULA

**Based on all patterns + Ana's essay framework + her existing tone:**

### Opening (2-3 paragraphs)

- **The spine:** What you believe, stated clearly
- **Make it tweetable:** This will become the quote everyone remembers
- **Example:** "The best GPs aren't prophets. They're pattern matchers with discipline."

### Arguments (3-5 sections)
- **Mix:** Narrative + data + insight
- **Use:** Proper nouns (real people, real deals, real numbers)
- **Structure:** Long ‚Üí short ‚Üí punch rhythm
- **Example section:**
  > "When Yellow Capital was raising $10M, we didn't pitch vision. We showed the IR infrastructure: pitch decks that converted, partner presentations that opened doors, outreach materials that got replies. The vision was in the execution. That's what LPs funded."

- **Weave:** Naturally into narrative, don't just list

When everyone flees the 'boring' markets, that's when generational companies get built. They always come crawling back. And when they do, you'll own what they're desperately trying to buy."
