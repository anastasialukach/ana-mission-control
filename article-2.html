Why Family Offices Are Building In-House Capital Formation Teams (And Hiring Aggressively) INTRO In March 2025, CFA Institute published research on Middle East family offices with one line that caught my attention: "Hiring aggressively to boost their capabilities." That's not normal language for a financial research report. "Aggressively" means something structural changed. Family offices in Dubai are no longer just allocating capital to external funds. They're building in-house capital formation teams - hiring people who used to work at: - PE/VC firms (IR roles) - Fund placement agents - Strategy consulting (Bain, McKinsey, BCG) - Investment banks (fundraising teams) I did IR for tech founders in Dubai, worked with family offices as allocators. This article breaks down why FOs are moving capital formation in-house - and what it means if you're in fundraising, IR, or capital raising. 1. The Economics Finally Make Sense The Old Model (Outsourced): - Family office has $500M AUM - Wants to raise external capital for co-investments - Hires placement agent at 2-3% of capital raised - Raises $100M ‚Üí pays $2-3M in fees The New Model (In-House): - Hire 2-3 capital formation professionals at $200K-$300K each - Annual cost: $600K-$900K (all-in comp + overhead) - They raise $100M ‚Üí $0 external fees - ROI breakeven: After first $30M-$40M raised What Changed: Family offices hitting critical mass ($500M+ AUM) where in-house teams are cheaper than outsourcing. The math flipped somewhere between 2023-2024. Real Example: Sirius Private Office (Dubai) is hiring for "Director, Capital Formation" roles. That's not a fund administrator position. That's building internal capability to raise capital without external agents. 2. Family Offices Are Going Direct (And Need IR Capability) The Trend: Family offices shifting from passive LP allocations to: - Direct investments (no fund intermediary) - Co-investments (alongside PE/VC funds) - Secondaries (buying LP stakes) CFA Institute (Mar 2025): "More family offices are allocating capital to private equity funds, direct investments, and venture capital opportunities." Why This Requires IR: When you're doing directs/co-invests at scale, you need: - Investor relations (managing co-investors) - Deal flow (sourcing opportunities) - Capital raising (for SPVs or fund vehicles) That's not something you outsource to a placement agent. That's an in-house function. The Talent Gap: Most family offices came from: - Real estate (property-focused) - Operating businesses (industrial/trading) - Oil wealth (passive allocations) None of those backgrounds teach you how to run institutional-quality IR. So they're hiring externally. 3. The "Institutionalization" Wave What's Happening: Middle East family offices maturing from: - "Family manages everything" ‚Üí Professional management teams - "Single LP in funds" ‚Üí Multi-family office structures - "Invest in friends' deals" ‚Üí Institutional due diligence CFA Institute Language: "Adopting more institutionalized governance." What That Means in Practice: - Compliance teams - Investment committees - IR reporting (quarterly letters, investor portals) - Capital formation (raising external capital properly) Who Gets Hired: People who've seen "good IR" at institutional funds. Ex-PE/VC IR folks who can build the function from scratch. The Dubai Angle: Dubai family offices are competing with Singapore/London FOs for deals. To compete, they need institutional credibility. That means professional IR teams. 4. The Consultant-to-FO Pipeline (This is Real) The National (Sept 2025): "People in strategy consulting are being hired by family offices from firms like Bain & Company, McKinsey and Boston Consulting Group." Why Consultants? - They speak "institutional investor" language - They build decks/models LPs expect - They understand governance structures - They can set up processes from zero The Career Path: - Consultant ‚Üí PE/VC firm (2-3 years) ‚Üí Family office capital formation lead - OR: Consultant ‚Üí Family office directly (if FO is sophisticated) Dubai Context: Cordell Partners (Dubai exec search firm) confirmed this trend. Family offices hiring externally from "different regions and sectors." That means: If you're doing IR in NY/London and open to Dubai, family offices want you. 5. The "Raise External Capital" Mandate What's Driving This: Family offices realizing they can: - Raise capital from other HNWIs (friends/network) - Structure as multi-family office (MFO) - Charge management fees (1-1.5% AUM) - Build recurring revenue business The Shift: From "we manage our family's money" ‚Üí "we manage multiple families' money and raise external capital." The National (Sept 2025): "Those dealing with raising external capital are also sought after." What This Requires: Someone who knows how to: - Build LP relationships - Run fundraising processes - Navigate regulatory (DIFC, SEC, MAS) - Structure fund vehicles That's a capital formation professional. Not an analyst. Not an advisor. Someone who's DONE fundraising at institutional scale. 6. Why Dubai Family Offices Specifically The Factors: 1. Tax Migration: HNW families relocating to Dubai (0% personal income tax) 2. Golden Visas: 10-year residency = long-term commitment 3. DIFC Infrastructure: Regulatory framework for multi-family offices 4. Capital Concentration: $500M+ families clustering in one city 5. Network Effects: More families = more co-invest opportunities The Result: Dubai has critical mass of family offices at the same time they're all professionalizing. That creates hiring waves. ME Family Office Summit: "More than 100 family offices from the Middle East will gather in Dubai." When 100+ FOs are in one city and they're all hiring similar roles, talent competition intensifies. 7. What This Means If You're in IR/Capital Formation The Opportunity: If you've done: - LP relations at a PE/VC fund - Fundraising at an investment bank - Capital formation at a placement agent - IR consulting Dubai family offices are hiring for these roles RIGHT NOW. Roles I'm Seeing: - Director, Capital Formation - Head of Investor Relations - VP, External Capital Raising - Managing Director, Family Office Partnerships Compensation: - $200K-$400K base (Dubai, tax-free) - Carry/bonuses tied to capital raised - Sometimes equity in the MFO structure The Trade-Offs: - Smaller teams (you're building from scratch) - Less structure (you define the playbook) - Higher impact (your work = direct ROI) CONCLUSION Family offices are moving capital formation in-house because: 1. Economics flipped (cheaper than outsourcing at scale) 2. Going direct requires IR capability 3. Institutionalization wave needs professional teams 4. Hiring ex-consultants who speak LP language 5. Raising external capital = recurring revenue 6. Dubai clustering accelerating trend 7. Talent gap = opportunity for external hires If you're in IR/fundraising: - Research family offices hiring in Dubai (Sirius, others) - Connect with exec search firms (Cordell Partners, etc.) - Understand multi-family office structures - Be ready to build, not just execute Or DM me - I'm exploring Director-level IR/capital formation roles in Dubai and happy to share what I've learned about the FO ecosystem.
